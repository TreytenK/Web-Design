Anthony Rendon

The Smooth Third Baseman

Early Life

Anthony Rendon was born on June 6, 1990, in Houston, Texas. From a young age, Rendon excelled in baseball, eventually becoming a standout player at Rice University, where he won multiple awards for his exceptional hitting and defense.

Professional Career

Rendon was drafted sixth overall in the 2011 MLB Draft by the Washington Nationals. He made his MLB debut in 2013 and quickly established himself as one of the league's premier third basemen.

Known for his clutch performances, Rendon played a key role in leading the Nationals to their first World Series championship in 2019. After the championship season, he signed with the Los Angeles Angels, continuing to showcase his all-around talent.

Achievements

  • World Series Champion (2019)
  • Silver Slugger Award (2019)
  • MLB All-Star (2019)
  • Led MLB in RBIs during the 2019 season

Personal Life

Off the field, Anthony Rendon is known for his humble and laid-back personality. He enjoys spending time with his family and giving back to the community through various charitable initiatives.
